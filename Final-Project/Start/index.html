Circulation Data for the Norfolk, VA Public Library System: What Can it Tell Us?

I am a reader and I come from a family of readers, which has led me to harbor a love for books and libraries, which are rooms and buildings full of books. While looking for a dataset to study, I searched the words "library" and "books" to see what the search site had to offer in topics I liked. This led me to the Norfolk Public Library System's Circulation Data. I have heard claims from various sources, thouhg most have been social media, that less and less people are reading, espcially physical media, so let's see what the data avlaable here says in regards to that.

The ciruclation data was collected for every month from July of 2016 to December of 2025. I had only looked at data from the moth of July for every year avalable, as I thouhgt it would be easier to analyse, but the difference between the graphs for only July and every month were so different that I decided to just look at all of the data together.

Total Materials Checked Out from July 2016 to December 2025

In the above graph, there is a clear dip around 2020, which can likely be attributed to Covid happening and the checking out of physical items being stopped. There is a massive recovery and the number of circulated materials rises above any recoded number since the data had begun to be kept.

On the other hand, the below graph with the July-specific numbers tells a different story. There is still a dip in 2020, but the recovery is much slower and less substancial as a whole.

Total Materials Checked out in July from 2026 to 2025

The below graph shows the total number of materials that have been checked out from each location that is a part of the Norfolk Public Library System. The Online portions of the system begin abruptly, as they seem to either not have offered that service until the general circulation data had laready begun to be recorded or that portion had just not yet been recorded. Most of the locations have similar numbers, but there are spikes in a few locations that may be able to be axplained by looking at the locations of the library building sin relation to each other and the surrounding area, which can also be seen below.

Total Materials Checked Out from July 2016 to December 2025 BY Location

The following map shows the locations of the library branches as they exist in the Norfolk area. There is a cluster of them that are within one mile of each other, which seems to give them the same amount of foot traffic for the most part. There is also one, the Pretlow branch, that is father away from the other branches and this has led it to be more active and have more materials to circulate. I have not looked specifically, but there could be a correlation between the library branch locations and schools, busniess areas, neighborhoods, or other community types being near those locations.

Map of Norfolk VA Public Library Branches

Overall, the Norfolk Public Library has several branches that loan out many, many materials year-round and while this took a hit during Covid, there seems to have been no permenant change to the number of materials that are circulated, other than a steady growth in those numbers. This also shows that, despite some beliefs that physical media is dying off and that reading is dying off, neither of those statements are true. Even if the number of non-physical media being checked out of libraries begins to exceed the number of physical media, that is still books being read/consumed and enjoyed, so it really is a win either way.
